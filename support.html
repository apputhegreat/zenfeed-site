Support

Help for Zenfeed users.

Zenfeed is a local Chrome extension for LinkedIn feed pages and X/twitter.com home timelines. This page explains the supported surfaces, common fixes, and what to include when reporting a problem.

Supported surfaces

  • LinkedIn feed pages: https://www.linkedin.com/feed/
  • X home timeline: https://x.com/home
  • Legacy Twitter home timeline: https://twitter.com/home

Zenfeed does not claim full support for LinkedIn jobs, LinkedIn search, LinkedIn profile pages, X search, X profile pages, or single-post pages.

Before reporting a bug

  1. Open chrome://extensions and confirm Zenfeed is enabled.
  2. Click the reload icon on the Zenfeed extension card after updating the extension.
  3. Refresh the LinkedIn feed or X home timeline tab.
  4. Open the Zenfeed toolbar popup and confirm the relevant filter is enabled.
  5. If a collapsed item is a false positive, click show on the restore bar.

Common questions

Does Zenfeed send my feed to a server?

No. Filtering runs locally in Chrome. The MVP has no backend, analytics, tracking pixel, cloud AI, or feed export.

Why did Zenfeed miss a promoted or suggested post?

LinkedIn and X change labels and page markup over time. Reports are most useful when they include the supported site, the filter that should have matched, and whether the missed item was on the feed/home surface.

Can I undo a hidden item?

In collapse mode, use the show button on the restore bar. You can also turn off a filter in the popup to restore matching items.

Report an issue

If Zenfeed is installed from the Chrome Web Store, use the support option on the Zenfeed store listing to report bugs, false positives, permission questions, and installation problems.

Report an issue via form ↗

Include:

  • The site: LinkedIn feed, X home, or twitter.com home.
  • The filter involved: promoted/ad, suggested, custom keyword, or Rest from AI.
  • Whether collapse mode was enabled.
  • The Chrome version and Zenfeed version.
  • A privacy-safe description of the issue. Do not include account credentials or private feed screenshots.
