Zenfeed vs SocialFocus

SocialFocus is a free multi-platform "focus tool" that hides feeds, recommendations, and distracting sections across five sites. It's the closest architectural match to Zenfeed — one extension, multiple platforms — but the two take different approaches to what gets filtered. Here's how they actually compare.

Side by side

Feature Zenfeed SocialFocus
Price Free, no tiers Free, with in-app purchases
Platforms LinkedIn, X/Twitter, YouTube YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, Reddit
Account required No No
AI-content detection Yes — Rest from AI keyword/phrase sweep, timed None
Promoted + Suggested filtering Yes, toggle per category Yes — promoted posts, plus feeds, recommended videos, Shorts, comments, sidebars, stories, and reels, per platform
Keyword blocking Yes — custom keyword list None
Timed auto-restore Yes — 15/30/60 min or a custom duration, everything comes back automatically when the timer ends No — has optional daily usage limits per site instead

Why someone might choose Zenfeed

Zenfeed covers LinkedIn, which SocialFocus doesn't touch at all, and adds two things SocialFocus doesn't offer: AI-content detection through Rest from AI and custom keyword blocking. Zenfeed is also fully free with no in-app purchases.

Why someone might still want SocialFocus

SocialFocus reaches more platforms overall — YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, and Reddit — and goes deeper on granular, per-platform hiding: recommended videos, Shorts, comments, sidebars, stories, and reels, with password-protected settings, import/export preferences, light/dark themes, and optional daily usage limits per site. It has a larger install base too (40,000 users, 4.6★ across 187 ratings) and works on both desktop and mobile web.

Source

SocialFocus facts above (pricing, platforms, ratings) are from its live Chrome Web Store listing, checked July 24, 2026: 4.6★ (187 ratings), 40,000 users, updated July 1, 2026.
