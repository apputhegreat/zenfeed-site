Zenfeed vs Clarity

Clarity (Hide AI Videos on YouTube) is a free, YouTube-only extension that auto-detects suspected AI-generated videos and improves its filtering over time through a community blocklist. Zenfeed covers LinkedIn, X, and YouTube and detects AI content locally through keyword matching. Here's how they actually compare.

Side by side

Feature Zenfeed Clarity
Price Free, no tiers Free
Platforms LinkedIn, X/Twitter, YouTube YouTube only
Account required No No
AI-content detection method Local keyword/phrase sweep — no account or data sharing needed Auto-detection plus an optional shared/community blocklist that improves filtering over time via crowdsourcing
Promoted + Suggested filtering Yes, toggle per category Not part of its stated feature set
Keyword blocking Yes — custom keyword list None
Timed auto-restore Yes — 15/30/60 min or a custom duration, everything comes back automatically when the timer ends No

Why someone might choose Zenfeed

Zenfeed covers LinkedIn and X in addition to YouTube, and adds promoted/suggested filtering and custom keyword blocking, none of which are part of Clarity's stated feature set. Zenfeed's AI detection is also entirely local — no account, and nothing about your feed gets shared to improve a shared model.

Why someone might still want Clarity

Clarity's community blocklist is a real, meaningful difference: users can label AI channels to contribute to a shared list that improves detection over time, something Zenfeed's local-only keyword approach doesn't do at all. Clarity is purpose-built for YouTube specifically, with a one-click toggle and a daily counter of videos blocked, on Home, Shorts, and recommendations. It's worth noting Clarity's install base is small — 1,000 users and 4.2★ from 10 ratings — so that track record is thinner than the other tools in this comparison series, and its developer states plainly that "filtering may not be perfect."
