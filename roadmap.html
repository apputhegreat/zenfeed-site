Roadmap

What we're fixing, what's next.

An honest list of what Zenfeed doesn't do well yet, and what's planned. No accounts, no tracking, no votes stored anywhere — just a page, kept up to date by hand.

  1. 1

    Occasionally a promoted or suggested post on LinkedIn isn't hidden right away. LinkedIn changes its page markup often, and detection can lag a little until it's updated.

  2. 2

    Rarely, an ordinary post can get hidden by mistake (a false positive). Click show on the restore bar to bring it back immediately — this is a known trade-off of local, rule-based filtering.

  3. 3

    Firefox isn't supported yet — Zenfeed is Chrome-only for now.

  4. 4

    On YouTube, only AI-recommended videos and keyword-blocked videos are filtered on Home, Subscriptions, and Search — the Shorts shelf and in-video/preroll ads aren't filtered yet.

  5. 5

    Zenfeed's AI detection matches AI-related keywords and phrases in a post or video's text (title, description, channel name) — it can't tell that an image or video is AI-generated just by looking at it if the creator doesn't say so. Purely visual or audio AI-generation detection isn't something Zenfeed does today.

  1. 1

    A filtered-item history log with one-click restore for anything Zenfeed hides — the most-requested control feature.

  2. 2

    Import/export for your custom keyword list, so you can back it up or move it to another computer.

  3. 3

    Author and topic rules — e.g. mute a specific person or company, not just keywords.

  4. 4

    A toggle to hide LinkedIn Company Page posts.

  5. 5

    A toggle to hide posts from LinkedIn Groups.

  6. 6

    A toggle to hide LinkedIn Newsletter posts.

  7. 7

    Per-category pause controls — pause just one filter temporarily, not everything.

  8. 8

    Firefox support, once Chrome usage and retention hold up.

  9. 9

    Filter by content type — hide reposts/shares, polls, or videos as their own category, separate from keyword or AI filtering.

  10. 10

    Hide posts older than a set age.

  11. 11

    Temporary keyword mutes — mute a keyword for a day or a week instead of only permanently, the same way Rest from AI works on a timer.

  12. 12

    An option to reduce algorithmic feed ordering on X — e.g. default to a more chronological view — as a separate lever from hiding specific posts.

How this page works

This is a static list we update by hand — there's no database behind it, no accounts, and no votes or comments stored anywhere. That's on purpose: Zenfeed itself is 100% local, with no backend and nothing exported off your device, and this page follows the same rule. If you want to weigh in on priority, use the report/suggest links above and it'll get read.
